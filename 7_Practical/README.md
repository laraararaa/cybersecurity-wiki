# 🧑‍💻 Practical Learning

Theory is important, but **cybersecurity is learned by doing**.

This section covers hands-on practice and real-world scenarios.

## What You'll Learn

1. [Lab Setup Guide](01_lab_setup.md) - Create your practice environment
2. [CTF Writeups](02_ctf_writeups.md) - Solve capture-the-flag challenges
3. [Practice Scenarios](03_scenarios.md) - Real-world situations

## Why Hands-On Practice?

- **Learning by doing**: You remember 75% of what you do vs 10% of what you read
- **Building muscle memory**: Security skills require practice
- **Safe environment**: Labs let you break things without consequences
- **Confidence**: Practice builds real-world readiness

## Learning Environment

You'll practice on:
- **Virtual machines**: Safe, isolated environments
- **Lab platforms**: HackTheBox, TryHackMe, etc.
- **CTF competitions**: Capture The Flag challenges
- **Your own lab**: Home lab setup

## Structure

For each challenge:
1. Read the scenario
2. Plan your approach
3. Execute your plan
4. Document what you did
5. Reflect on what you learned

## Important: Stay Legal

- Only practice on systems you own or have permission for
- Use isolated environments
- Never attack real systems without authorization
- Follow responsible disclosure
